PROJECT SUMMARY

I discovered employees entering data and auditing the bulk grocery section was consuming valuable employee time.

Department managers from twenty stores needed a streamlined global system to audit and input bulk grocery PLUs into the Aisle iQ database.

After several interviews, I observed that the tag could not be successfully scanned and thus had to be typed manually.

My redesign included… removal of superfluous tag information updated barcode that successfully scanned for faster, more efficient data entry

The redesign resulted in… 100% scanning accuracy versus previous 96% weekly labor savings companywide of 13.33 hours annual labor savings companywide of $12,458.33

Overview

My Role: UX and Service Designer

CHALLENGE & GOAL

Challenge

User interviews uncovered that the barcodes on the existing tags in bulk grocery were not properly formatted, and when scanned they did not generate the correct data for the auditing and Aisle iQ apps.

Thus, users needed to type each individual PLU into the hand scanner which significantly increased labor hours and caused employee frustration.

Goal

My goal was to create a barcode that would generate the correct data when scanned for the auditing and Aisle iQ apps.

In addition, I needed to develop the installation procedure and use of the new barcodes on the bulk grocery tags to reduce labor costs.

Current Situation

The old tag design included a barcode that did not produce correct data when scanned, a confusing layout, and seven pieces of unnecessary information.

This created a confusing user experience with overwhelming information when scanning and ordering.

User Journey

Interviews with stakeholders, developers and users illustrated that inputting the bulk codes by typing numbers on a hand scanner took approximately three hours. Many users also experienced the hand scanner crashing during the process, which caused frustration.

The high volume of information on the current tag caused users to make ordering and auditing mistakes with a 95 percent accuracy rating.

DESIGN

The redesigned tag incorporates a smaller barcode that generates correct data, with a cleaner layout where extraneous details have been omitted.

The E pattern presents information that is easily digestible by users.

Sleek and streamlined design

Tag Placement

The first iteration: tag barcodes placed on the side of the bulk bins.

Current placement was based on quick and easy tag installation.

This location proved challenging because when bins were cleaned or dry goods were changed, the barcodes were scrubbed off and stickers would need to be reapplied.

The tag on the side of the bin wore out quickly from cleaning and filling the bins

The second iteration: below where barcodes were placed in the middle of the Trade Label. Stickers no longer needed to be reapplied, but a new problem emerged. The close proximity of the order barcode and PLU barcode caused scanning issues.

The scanner might accidentally scan the opposing barcode since they are so close

The solution: to place the barcode at the bottom right corner of the Trade Label. This allowed easy scanning of the PLU barcode and only covered the effective date of the trade label, which was unimportant information.

Notice the barcode is small enough that it does not cover up nutritional information

CONCLUSION

The new tag significantly reduced the time spent typing numbers by hand. Auditing increased to 100% accuracy while ordering increased to 99% accuracy.

The updated tag includes strategically-placed bar codes that populate correct information when scanned, easily transferred to the Aisle iQ and Audit apps.
